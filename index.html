About the Crop:

Pusa 23 is a high-yielding variety of pearl millet developed for dryland farming. It thrives in sandy loam to loamy soils with good drainage, requiring minimal water—around 300-400 mm throughout its growth cycle. Pusa 23 has a per acre production potential of 12-15 quintals under optimal conditions. It's drought-tolerant, making it ideal for rain-fed areas. The crop's low water requirement and ability to grow in less fertile soils contribute to its cost-effectiveness.

  • Soil Composition: best in sandy loam to loamy soils with PH of 6.0 and 7.5
  • Water need per Acre: 0.24 to 0.33 feet
  • Potential Dangers: Climate Variability, Water Stress, Pest Attacks
  • Fertilizers: Phosphorus, Zinc Sulphate, Potassium, Nitrogen
  • Pesticides: Carbofuran 3G, Spinosad 45 SC, Dimethoate 30 EC
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹18,000 to ₹37,000
